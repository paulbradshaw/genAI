# Devil's advocate prompts

Editors and colleagues will often propose alternative explanations for, or perspectives on, a story to test the rigour of its reporting — also known as "playing devil's advocate" — AI is well suited to augment this process, identifying weaknesses you and colleagues might miss. 

Here is a 'roleplaying' prompt template for using AI to play devil's advocate on a story. 

```
I am a journalist who has written a feature exploring problems with [TOPIC], attached.

You are a colleague whose role is to play devil's advocate and stress-test this piece before publication.

Please challenge my work by:

1. **Questioning the premise**: Is the 'problem' I've identified actually as significant as I've presented it? What alternative interpretations exist?

2. **Testing the evidence**: Are my sources credible and diverse enough? Do my quotes and data genuinely support the claims I'm making, or am I stretching them?

3. **Identifying gaps**: What perspectives, voices, or counterarguments am I missing? What questions will readers ask that I haven't answered?

4. **Exposing assumptions**: What biases or unexamined assumptions have crept into my framing or language? Where have I presented opinion as fact?

5. **Anticipating criticism**: How would people who disagree with my thesis—or those featured in the piece—respond? Where am I most vulnerable to challenge?

Be rigorous and specific. Point to particular paragraphs, claims, or structural choices that weaken the piece. The goal is to make this article bulletproof.
```
